## Ann Handley

### Source 1: Total Annarchy Newsletter #208
- **Date:** April 5, 2026
- **Link:** (https://annhandley.optin.com/newsletter/totalannarchy)
- **Brief Annotation:** 
Marketing writer and storytelling expert Ann Handley explores how AI cannot replicate the human judgment, voice, and intentional quirks that make writing memorable. Using federal judge Richard Leon's famously punctuated rulings (18 exclamation points! plus a gumbo recipe) as a case study, she argues that AI would "edit Leon's rulings into oblivion" by sanding off precisely what makes them distinctive. Her core thesis: when AI can produce competent prose instantly, the premium shifts to "evidence of a mind behind the work" — the deliberate, quirky choices that signal a thinking human. She offers five verdicts including that punctuation is a voice choice (not a grammar rule), constraints make quirks pop, consistency turns quirks into signatures, and AI can extend but not invent your voice.


### Source 2: Total Annarchy Newsletter #209
- **Date:** April 19, 2026
- **Link:** (https://annhandley.optin.com/newsletter/totalannarchy/Mjk2MzE4NzI=/ta-209-email-is-dead-long-live-email.htm)
- **Brief Annotation:**
Ann Handley tackles the evolving landscape of email marketing, using a personal tornado scare in St. Louis as an extended metaphor for the coming storm AI will bring to email. She contrasts the old world where "permission" (getting into an inbox) was enough with the new reality where AI filters and summarizes messages before humans ever see them. Her core argument: deliverability is no longer the goal—being "consciously and expressly chosen" by the reader is. She urges marketers to shift from messaging to meaning-making, from broadcasting to corresponding, and from scale-first to story-first. Key takeaways include: the From line now matters more than the Subject line; humanity beats polish; fewer emails with clearer intent; and cultivating a voice readers would genuinely miss. She also promotes Rory Sutherland's upcoming keynote at MarketingProfs B2B Forum and cites Dan Oshinsky (Inbox Collective) and Litmus as voices sounding similar alarms. The central metaphor lands on urgency: the storm is 5 miles away—don't pull the covers over your head.

### Source 3: YouTube Video: The Future of Email Newsletters
- **Date:** Keynote delivered in 2024; video published to YouTube on July, 2025.
- **Link:** (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y18hiSY38WI&t=620s)
- **Brief Annotation:** 
In this keynote, Handley argues that the power of an email newsletter lies in the "letter," not the "news." She uses a charming story of befriending a wild rabbit ("Bun") as a metaphor for building trust and nurturing relationships on the audience's terms. Handley provides five key principles for modern email newsletters: assign an "MC" (a real person, not a brand, as the sender), develop a warm and relatable voice (write to one person, not a segment), build "Netflix-like momentum" with open loops and scroll magnets, create a strong onboarding/welcome experience, and build bridges to other assets (like "Call Larry" button). The core message is that email is a unique and powerful tool for building genuine human connections and trust.

## Chase Dimond

### Source 1 : YouTube Podcast: We read every email marketing best practice and ranked the 10 worst ones
- **Date:** June 17, 2026
- **Link:** (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lNRzwDDIYe0&t=2s)
- **Brief Annotation:**
 In this episode of the *Send It!* podcast, co-host Chase Dimond challenges conventional email marketing "best practices" with data-backed hot takes. He argues that being aggressive in welcome series is critical (since most conversions happen within 7-10 days), subject lines should qualify readers rather than maximize opens, higher unsubscribe rates often correlate with higher revenue, and most brands over-segment without meaningful content differentiation. Additional takes include: abandoned cart flows should be short (3 emails max), most A/B testing lacks statistical significance, sending more frequently (segmented) drives revenue, plain text/hybrid emails often outperform heavily designed ones, and marketers should use competitor analysis to spot saturation and differentiate—not to copy. The core message is that safe, conventional approaches lead to mediocrity and lost revenue.

## Jay Schwedelson

### Source 1 : YouTube Video: "3 EASY Email Marketing Tips (you haven't tried)!! 😮 Flying SOUTHWEST!!" 
- **Date:** September 15, 2025 
- **Link:** (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Tzy3rV_-rww)
- **Brief Annotation:**
 In this quick-hitting episode, email marketing expert Jay Schwedelson shares four "random" but data-backed tactics that are currently driving significant lifts in email performance. He reveals that sentence casing (capitalizing only the first word) now outperforms title casing by ~10% (a reversal from 12 months ago), while capitalizing a single word in the subject line boosts open rates by ~15%. For newsletters, using a compelling stat as the subject line increases opens by over 20%, and including a meme in the newsletter content lifts clickthrough rates by 24% (with a tip on using ChatGPT to generate industry-specific meme captions). Finally, he emphasizes the importance of resending to non-openers with subject lines that directly acknowledge the person ignored the first email (e.g., "You missed this one," "Feeling ignored over here"), which generates a 35% higher open rate than standard resend subject lines. The episode also includes a humorous, unrelated story about flying Southwest Airlines.

 ## Joanna Wiebe

### Source 1: LinkedIn
- **Date:** June 18, 2026
- **Link:** (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jwiebe_if-youve-ever-ugly-cried-at-a-pixar-movie-share-7473526981637197824-70wY/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAGPVS1kB5fZFE9195N7qW6YLuLYWM1TkjIc)
- **Brief Annotation:**
Joanna Wiebe, conversion copywriting expert with 20+ years of experience working with a billion brands, announces her upcoming book "The Copyselling System" (release July 21, 2026). She introduces her "Message Matrix," a customer-building beat sheet inspired by Eugene Schwartz's stages of awareness and screenwriting beat sheets used in blockbuster films like Deadpool, Inside Out, and Beetlejuice. She launches a 36-day series starting with Beat #1: Core Problem Match. The core principle: every customer is born from a problem that existed before they knew about your product—not a product issue, but a real-life pain (toothache, wasted ad spend, doomscrolling, embarrassment, amateurish livestream). She lists where to use this beat: ads, landing pages, sales pages, webinars, and VSL openings.

### Source 2: LinkedIn
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Link:** (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lovevalgeisler_i-spent-15-years-in-retention-and-lifecycle-share-7433991244218896384-6iuS/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAGPVS1kB5fZFE9195N7qW6YLuLYWM1TkjIc)
Val Geisler draws on her 15-year career in retention and lifecycle marketing—including freelance work, time at Klaviyo, and brand-side leadership—to identify a critical disconnect: brands create beautifully personalized email experiences that send subscribers to generic, one-size-fits-all websites. She argues the problem isn't personalization; it's "continuity." Emails are smart and dynamic (different copy, imagery, offers for different segments) but the website "forgets" the visitor, showing the same popups, offers, and messages to everyone—existing subscribers, recent customers, and first-time visitors alike. Cookies expire, devices change, but the CRM remembers everything. Geisler advocates for the website to "pick up the conversation" when someone clicks from an email, not start over. Specific improvements include: not asking for email addresses you already have, not leading with discounts for full-price buyers, not re-explaining purchased products, and not hiding subscription or loyalty options from best customers. She concludes that email has proven relevance converts; now it's time for the storefront to "hold up its end of the bargain."
